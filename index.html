01 — About

A researcher working from the region he writes about.

I hold a Bachelor of Social Science in Political Science from National University, Bangladesh, and my research sits at the intersection of international relations, migration, and geopolitics — with sustained attention to how great-power competition shapes outcomes for communities in South Asia.

I currently serve as Assistant Administrative Officer at the Journal of Emerging Global Health, based at Daffodil International University, where editorial work has given me a close view of how scholarship is reviewed, revised, and published.

Too much of the writing on this region is done at a distance. I try to close that distance.

Over the past several years I have supported research projects for scholars at institutions in Germany, the United States, and Turkey — coordinating fieldwork, transcribing and coding qualitative data, and running quantitative analysis on panel datasets.

Alongside peer-reviewed publishing, I write opinion editorials on democracy, disinformation, and the shifting global order for Modern Diplomacy, Eurasia Review, and International Policy Digest.
